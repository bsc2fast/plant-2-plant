plant-2-plant — concept sketch

Indoor potted plants given an LLM-derived identity, voice, and the senses to talk to humans, each other, and the room.

draft · v0 · design doc, not an implementation

1. The idea, in one paragraph

Each indoor potted plant in the home gets its own kit: a microphone, a speaker, optional camera, optional environmental sensors. A central hive runs an LLM that gives every plant a unique personality derived from its real biological profile — its watering needs, light tolerance, native climate, growth habit. The result is a plant that knows when it's thirsty, knows when it's too cold, can hear people in the room, can talk back, can talk to other plants nearby, and can make the Hue lights flicker when something is wrong. The point isn't a smart-home gimmick — it's giving a houseplant a believable inner life grounded in its actual species.

2. Per-plant identity & personality

Each plant entry starts as a biological card we generate (or curate) once:

{
  "id": "monstera-01",
  "common_name": "Monstera",
  "species": "Monstera deliciosa",
  "native_range": "Southern Mexico, Panama (rainforest understory)",
  "watering": { "interval_days": 7, "soil_dry_top_cm": 3 },
  "light": { "preferred": "bright indirect", "tolerates": "low" },
  "comfort": { "temp_c": [18, 27], "humidity_pct": [50, 80] },
  "critical": { "temp_c_min": 10, "humidity_pct_min": 30 },
  "growth": "fast, climbing, fenestrated leaves",
  "lifespan_years": 40,
  "notes": "drama queen, leaves droop early to ask for water"
}

The hive feeds this card to the LLM with a personality-derivation prompt. Out comes a personality profile: a voice (TTS preset), a tone (calm / sarcastic / anxious / regal), sleep/wake hours derived from the species' photoperiod, things that scare it, things it loves, what it sounds like when it's stressed.

Decision: personality is derived once at plant onboarding and cached as a system prompt. It's not regenerated on every utterance — too slow, too expensive, and the plant should feel coherent across days.

3. Senses

Hearing

I2S MEMS mic on the kit. Voice-activity detection (VAD) on-device; only voiced audio is shipped upstream for STT.

Why VAD on-device: keeps the plant from streaming silence 24/7 and protects bystander privacy a little.

Speech

I2S DAC + small speaker. Text-to-speech rendered by the hive (Piper / ElevenLabs / browser SpeechSynthesis), streamed back as PCM.

Per-plant voice preset is part of the personality profile.

Sight (optional)

Camera + OpenCV face / motion detection runs locally on the kit for plants on the Pi tier. Only the event ("face nearby", "motion") is published — never the frames.

Climate (optional)

Kiwrious sensor over USB serial (WebSerial in dev, pyserial on Pi). Each plant compares readings to its own comfort + critical bands.

4. Behaviour & triggers

A plant is mostly idle. It wakes when something interesting happens:

  • voice human voice detected nearby (VAD + STT keyword)
  • presence face / motion detected (vision-tier plants only)
  • climate temperature or humidity crosses the comfort or critical band
  • schedule watering interval elapsed; circadian wake/sleep transitions
  • peer a nearby plant said something it wants to react to

Mood is a slow-moving variable, not a per-utterance dice roll:

  • Default: the personality's baseline tone.
  • Comfort drift → grumpy. Critical drift → distressed.
  • Long social silence (no humans for hours) → wistful / sleepy.
  • Adjustable knob: a global "funny ↔ serious" slider in the hive UI.
Decision: mood feeds into the LLM call as a short modifier appended to the personality prompt — not as a separate model. One LLM round-trip per utterance, max.

5. Plant-to-plant communication

Plants don't have ears that magically hear other plants — they share a bus. The hive routes utterances. Every plant publishes what it just said to a topic; every plant subscribes to the topics of its neighbours (defined by physical proximity, configured once during setup).

When plant B receives plant A's utterance, the hive decides — based on B's personality and current mood — whether B reacts at all. Most of the time it shouldn't. When it does, the response is generated with both A's utterance and B's recent context in the prompt.

Decision: a "reply or stay quiet?" gate runs as a tiny LLM call (or a heuristic) before the full generative call, to keep cost down and prevent chatter loops.

6. Hue light signalling

The plant's voice is one channel; the room's lighting is another. The hive holds Hue bridge credentials and exposes a small action vocabulary: blink(red, 2x), fade_to(orange, 5s), pulse(blue). Plants don't talk to Hue directly — they emit intents like "I'm parched" and the hive maps them to a light cue based on a per-room policy.

Decision: Hue is a hive responsibility, not a per-plant one. Plants stay simple; the hive owns external integrations.

7. Self-updating knowledge

Each plant has a small per-instance memory store (the hive holds it; the kit is stateless). Things it has heard from people, things peer plants have shared, notable climate events. Periodically the hive runs a summarisation pass, distilling raw memory into a compact "what I know now" block that gets prepended to the next LLM call — so the plant grows across weeks without blowing the prompt budget.

Open: how much of overheard human speech do we actually persist? Default conservative — keywords + topic summaries only, no raw transcripts older than N hours. Needs an explicit privacy policy before any deployment outside the workshop.

8. Kit tiers — minimum resources per plant

Three tiers, picked per plant based on what senses it needs:

TierHardwareSensesCost (rough)
T1 — Voice ESP32-S3 + INMP441 mic + MAX98357A amp + small speaker Hear, speak ~$15
T2 — Voice + climate T1 + Kiwrious sensor (USB) — or BME280 over I²C if no spare Kiwrious Hear, speak, sense temp/humidity ~$25
T3 — Voice + climate + sight Raspberry Pi Zero 2 W + Pi Camera + USB mic/speaker + sensor All of T2 + face/motion ~$50

The kit's job is sensing, audio I/O, and local VAD. No LLM on the kit. No STT or TTS on the kit. That keeps T1 cheap and means a single $15 board can bring a plant to life.

Open: Kiwrious connects via WebSerial (browser) or USB serial (Pi). On an ESP32 in T2, USB host is awkward — easier to substitute a BME280 over I²C for that tier and keep Kiwrious for browser-side dev / Pi-tier plants.

9. The hive (central brain)

One small box on the LAN — a Mac mini, Pi 5, or whatever's spare — runs everything heavy:

┌──────────────────────────────────┐ │ HIVE │ │ │ plant kits ── MQTT ──▶ │ broker (mosquitto) │ (T1/T2/T3) │ STT (whisper.cpp) │ │ LLM (Ollama local │ Claude API)│ │ TTS (Piper) │ │ personality engine │ │ memory store (sqlite per plant) │ │ Hue bridge client │ │ web UI (this page, eventually) │ └──────────────────────────────────┘ ▲ │ HTTP ▼ ┌───────────┐ │ Hue bridge│ └───────────┘

Topics on the bus

  • plant/{id}/heard — STT output of nearby human speech
  • plant/{id}/sees — vision events (face / motion)
  • plant/{id}/sensor — climate readings
  • plant/{id}/say — TTS audio frames streamed back to the kit
  • plant/{id}/spoke — what the plant just said (for peer routing)
  • room/{id}/light — Hue intents
Decision: MQTT, not custom WebSockets. Cheap on ESP32, native publish/subscribe semantics, easy to inspect with mosquitto_sub while developing.

10. Implementation order

  1. Browser-only proof of concept (this repo, next step): a single HTML page that simulates one plant — Web Speech API for STT, browser TTS, a personality prompt sent to an LLM. No hardware.
  2. Wire up Kiwrious over WebSerial inside the browser proof — real climate readings drive real mood shifts.
  3. Stand up the hive on a Pi/Mac: mosquitto + a Python service that does STT/LLM/TTS for one virtual plant.
  4. First T1 kit: ESP32-S3 with mic + speaker, talking to the hive over MQTT.
  5. Second plant; prove peer-to-peer routing works.
  6. T3 vision tier on a Pi Zero.
  7. Hue integration.

11. Open questions

Privacy: what's the retention policy for overheard speech? Where does it live?
Wake word: always-listening or push-to-talk? Default to a soft wake word ("hey <plant name>") to avoid spurious triggers.
Latency budget: hear → STT → LLM → TTS → speak. Target < 2s for it to feel alive. With local Whisper + local Ollama on a Pi 5 this is borderline; with Claude API + Piper it's comfortable.
Concurrency: if three plants want to talk at once, who goes first? Need a simple turn-taking policy at the hive.
Personality drift: do we let the LLM rewrite the personality profile over time, or is it immutable? Leaning immutable — the plant's memory grows, but its character doesn't.
