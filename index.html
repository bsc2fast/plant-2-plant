0.What kind of thing is this?

Project type, before implementation.

plant-2-plant is a speculative design project [42] — an artifact-as-argument, closer to a diegetic prototype [43] than a product. It sits inside the more-than-human design tradition [18][19], asking what happens when the addressed subject of the interface is the plant rather than the human [21][20]. The contribution is an arrangement: hardware kits, an LLM hive, and a set of personas grounded in real species biology.

Honesty footnote. Whether plants "have" cognition or experience is contested in plant science [2][51]. We do not need to settle that to proceed: an LLM persona is performance, not identity [26]. We borrow the shape of the science to compose voices we find legible — and we say so.

1.The idea, in one paragraph

The whole project, compressed.

Each indoor potted plant in the home gets its own kit: a microphone, a speaker, optional camera, optional environmental sensors. A central hive runs an LLM that gives every plant a unique personality derived from its real biological profile — its watering needs, light tolerance, native climate, growth habit. The result is a plant that knows when it's thirsty, knows when it's too cold, can hear people in the room, can talk back, can talk to other plants nearby [7][6], and can make the Hue lights flicker when something is wrong. The point isn't a smart-home gimmick — it's giving a houseplant a believable inner life grounded in its actual species, through a calm interaction profile [34][36] rather than another always-on assistant.

Lineage — what this builds on

Botanicalls [11] → I/O Plant [14] → Botanicus Interacticus [10] → Florence [13] → plant-2-plant

Each step adds one capacity. Botanicalls let the plant reach out when thirsty. Florence gave it conversational form. We add: a persona that's different per species, peer-to-peer talk between plants, and an LLM brain that lets the conversation be open-ended rather than scripted. We deliberately do not follow the biopotential-to-MIDI sonification lineage [16] — language, not sound art, because semantic legibility is the point.

2.Per-plant identity & personality

From a biological card to a stable LLM persona.

Each plant entry starts as a biological card we generate (or curate) once:

{
  "id": "monstera-01",
  "common_name": "Monstera",
  "species": "Monstera deliciosa",
  "native_range": "Southern Mexico, Panama (rainforest understory)",
  "watering": { "interval_days": 7, "soil_dry_top_cm": 3 },
  "light": { "preferred": "bright indirect", "tolerates": "low" },
  "comfort": { "temp_c": [18, 27], "humidity_pct": [50, 80] },
  "critical": { "temp_c_min": 10, "humidity_pct_min": 30 },
  "growth": "fast, climbing, fenestrated leaves",
  "lifespan_years": 40,
  "notes": "drama queen, leaves droop early to ask for water"
}

The hive feeds this card to the LLM with a personality-derivation prompt. Out comes a personality profile: a voice (TTS preset), a tone (calm / sarcastic / anxious / regal), sleep/wake hours derived from the species' photoperiod, things that scare it, things it loves, what it sounds like when it's stressed. Persona stability over time is borrowed from the generative-agents architecture [25].

Decision: personality is derived once at plant onboarding and cached as a system prompt. Memory grows; character does not. This matches the role-play framing — coherence over weeks comes from a stable role, not from the model "becoming" the plant [26].

3.Senses

What the kit perceives, and what stays on-device.

Hearing

I2S MEMS mic on the kit. Voice-activity detection (VAD) on-device; only voiced audio is shipped upstream for STT.

VAD on-device because shipping continuous room audio to the hive recreates exactly the smart-speaker privacy problem [40]. TinyML on ESP32-class boards makes wake-word and VAD feasible at the edge [49][50].

Speech

I2S DAC + small speaker. Text-to-speech rendered by the hive (Piper [47] for local, ElevenLabs/cloud as alternative), streamed back as PCM.

Per-plant voice preset is part of the personality profile.

Sight (optional)

Camera + OpenCV face / motion detection runs locally on the kit for plants on the Pi tier. Only the event ("face nearby", "motion") is published — never the frames.

Same privacy logic as hearing — events leave the kit, raw sensing does not.

Climate (optional)

Kiwrious sensor over USB serial (WebSerial in dev, pyserial on Pi). Each plant compares readings to its own comfort + critical bands.

Climate drift is the trigger that grounds the loudest claim — that drought stress is communicable [1].

4.Behaviour & triggers

Mostly idle. Occasionally interesting.

A plant is mostly idle. This is the design — the calm-tech profile [34] rather than the always-listening assistant [38]. It wakes when something interesting happens:

  • voice human voice detected nearby (VAD + STT keyword)
  • presence face / motion detected (vision-tier plants only)
  • climate temperature or humidity crosses the comfort or critical band
  • schedule watering interval elapsed; circadian wake/sleep transitions
  • peer a nearby plant said something it wants to react to

Mood is a slow-moving variable, not a per-utterance dice roll, drawing on tangible-mood precedent [17]:

  • Default: the personality's baseline tone.
  • Comfort drift → grumpy. Critical drift → distressed.
  • Long social silence (no humans for hours) → wistful / sleepy.
  • Adjustable knob: a global "funny ↔ serious" slider in the hive UI.
Decision: mood feeds into the LLM call as a short modifier appended to the personality prompt — not as a separate model. One LLM round-trip per utterance, max.

5.Plant-to-plant communication

A shared bus, deliberately bounded by proximity.

Plants don't have ears that magically hear other plants — they share a bus. The hive routes utterances. Every plant publishes what it just said to a topic; every plant subscribes to the topics of its neighbours (defined by physical proximity, configured once during setup). The proximity bound is intentional: real plant-to-plant signalling via volatiles is a local phenomenon [7][8], and we honour that shape rather than letting every plant overhear every other.

When plant B receives plant A's utterance, the hive decides — based on B's personality and current mood — whether B reacts at all. Most of the time it shouldn't. When it does, the response is generated with both A's utterance and B's recent context in the prompt. The mycorrhizal-network framing [5][6] is a useful analogy for the routing layer: a shared substrate carrying signals across an ecology.

Decision: a "reply or stay quiet?" gate runs as a tiny LLM call (or a heuristic) before the full generative call, to keep cost down and prevent chatter loops.

6.Hue light signalling

Speech for moments worth interrupting; light for everything else.

The plant's voice is one channel; the room's lighting is another. The hive holds Hue bridge credentials and exposes a small action vocabulary: blink(red, 2x), fade_to(orange, 5s), pulse(blue). Plants don't talk to Hue directly — they emit intents like "I'm parched" and the hive maps them to a light cue based on a per-room policy. Light becomes the ambient channel that doesn't interrupt — the speech channel for moments worth interrupting [36].

Decision: Hue is a hive responsibility, not a per-plant one. Plants stay simple; the hive owns external integrations.

7.Self-updating knowledge

Memory grows; character does not.

Each plant has a small per-instance memory store (the hive holds it; the kit is stateless). Things it has heard from people, things peer plants have shared, notable climate events. Periodically the hive runs a summarisation pass — the reflection step from generative agents [25] — distilling raw memory into a compact "what I know now" block that gets prepended to the next LLM call [27]. The plant grows across weeks without blowing the prompt budget. Crucially, the personality prompt itself is not rewritten; only memory grows.

Open: how much of overheard human speech do we actually persist? Default conservative — keywords + topic summaries only, no raw transcripts older than N hours [40]. Needs an explicit privacy policy before any deployment outside the workshop.

8.Kit tiers — minimum resources per plant

Three tiers picked per plant by what senses it needs.

TierHardwareSensesCost
T1 — Voice ESP32-S3 + INMP441 mic + MAX98357A amp + small speaker Hear, speak ~$15
T2 — Voice + climate T1 + Kiwrious sensor (USB) — or BME280 over I²C if no spare Kiwrious Hear, speak, sense temp/humidity ~$25
T3 — Voice + climate + sight Raspberry Pi Zero 2 W + Pi Camera + USB mic/speaker + sensor All of T2 + face/motion ~$50

The kit's job is sensing, audio I/O, and local VAD. No LLM on the kit. No STT or TTS on the kit. That keeps T1 cheap and means a single $15 board can bring a plant to life. The "I/O plant" toolkit lineage [14] is what we extend here — a kit-per-pot rather than a single shared appliance.

Open: Kiwrious connects via WebSerial (browser) or USB serial (Pi). On an ESP32 in T2, USB host is awkward — easier to substitute a BME280 over I²C for that tier and keep Kiwrious for browser-side dev / Pi-tier plants.

9.The hive (central brain)

One LAN box does all the heavy work.

One small box on the LAN — a Mac mini, Pi 5, or whatever's spare — runs everything heavy. The architecture mirrors the perception–brain–action loop common to LLM-agent systems [29][28]:

┌──────────────────────────────────┐ │ HIVE │ │ │ plant kits ── MQTT ──▶ │ broker (mosquitto) │ (T1/T2/T3) │ STT (whisper.cpp) │ │ LLM (Ollama local │ Claude API)│ │ TTS (Piper) │ │ personality engine │ │ memory store (sqlite per plant) │ │ Hue bridge client │ │ web UI (this page, eventually) │ └──────────────────────────────────┘ ▲ │ HTTP ▼ ┌───────────┐ │ Hue bridge│ └───────────┘

Concrete software choices: Whisper for STT [45][46], Ollama for local LLM [48], Piper for local TTS [47]. All run comfortably on a Pi 5 or Mac mini.

Topics on the bus

  • plant/{id}/heard — STT output of nearby human speech
  • plant/{id}/sees — vision events (face / motion)
  • plant/{id}/sensor — climate readings
  • plant/{id}/say — TTS audio frames streamed back to the kit
  • plant/{id}/spoke — what the plant just said (for peer routing)
  • room/{id}/light — Hue intents
Decision: MQTT, not custom WebSockets. Cheap on ESP32, native publish/subscribe semantics, easy to inspect with mosquitto_sub while developing.

10.Implementation order

Browser proof first; hardware later.

  1. Browser-only proof of concept (this repo, next step): a single HTML page that simulates one plant — Web Speech API for STT, browser TTS, a personality prompt sent to an LLM. No hardware.
  2. Wire up Kiwrious over WebSerial inside the browser proof — real climate readings drive real mood shifts.
  3. Stand up the hive on a Pi/Mac: mosquitto + a Python service that does STT/LLM/TTS for one virtual plant.
  4. First T1 kit: ESP32-S3 with mic + speaker, talking to the hive over MQTT.
  5. Second plant; prove peer-to-peer routing works.
  6. T3 vision tier on a Pi Zero.
  7. Hue integration.

11.Critical reflection & open questions

What we have not answered, and what we should worry about.

Adding a voice to a houseplant is not neutral. People will treat it as a person, easily and quickly — that's the CASA effect [30][31][32]. Designing for that response without examining its risks is irresponsible [33][24]. The persona we generate from a species card encodes someone's idea of what that plant is like — likely the LLM's training-distribution average, filtered through our prompts. That's worth being honest about.

The questions we have not answered:

Privacy: what's the retention policy for overheard speech? Where does it live? [40]
Wake word: always-listening or push-to-talk? Default to a soft wake word ("hey <plant name>") to avoid spurious triggers [41].
Latency budget: hear → STT → LLM → TTS → speak. Target < 2s for it to feel alive. With local Whisper + local Ollama on a Pi 5 this is borderline; with Claude API + Piper it's comfortable.
Concurrency: if three plants want to talk at once, who goes first? Need a simple turn-taking policy at the hive [41].
Personality drift: do we let the LLM rewrite the personality profile over time, or is it immutable? Leaning immutable — the plant's memory grows, but its character doesn't [26].
Voice authorship: whose idea of "Monstera personality" are we encoding? The LLM's, the curator's, the cultural-stereotype average? [24]
Standing: do these plants get a say? If "Nation of Plants" [4][52] is taken seriously, the entity we are building is a representative — and representatives can be wrong on behalf of the represented.
